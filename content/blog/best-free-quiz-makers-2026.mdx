## 2. Google Forms

Google Forms is one of the most widely used tools, but it is very basic compared to modern quiz platforms.

### Key features:

- Easy to share  
- Simple question types  
- Basic response tracking

### Best for:

## 3. Kahoot

Kahoot focuses more on gamified learning and classroom engagement.

### Key features:
- Fun game-like quiz experience  
- Students enjoy participation  
- Live classroom competitions  
- Interactive UI  

### Limitations:
- Not ideal for deep assessments  
- Limited customization  
- Internet dependent for live play  

### Best for:
Engaging students in classroom activities.
